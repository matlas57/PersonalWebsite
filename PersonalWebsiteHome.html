About me
Hi, my name is Matan. I am a passionate engineer that enjoys creating, learning, and optimizing. My passion for programming started at the National High School Game Academy at Carnegie Mellon University in 2018. From the projects there I fell in love with the process of building and improving, seeing my creations improve incrementally inspired me to keep working towards my goals. This love for improving then spread to all aspects of my life, motivating me to work every day to improve one aspect of my life each and every day.

Projects
Below are some of my favorite projects I've worked on in the past few years
Splendor
Splendor is a card game where players compete to build Renaissance Europe's most lucrative jewelry business by acquiring gems, purchasing mines, and gaining the favor of nobles. This implementation of Splendor was implemented with a Java backend and a JavaFX frontend, we used technologies such as REST, Docker, Maven, and Git in developing it as part of our year-long project for COMP 361 at McGill University. Feel free to try it out for yourself here.
Ray Tracer
One of my favorite courses in university has been Fundamentals of Computer Graphics. Learning about all the processes that take place to bring a coded image onto a screen has transformed the way I think about TV, movies, and games. This ray tracer project is an implementation of ray tracing technology that has become so prevalent in graphics today. Feel free to explore some sample images and the code behind them here.
Sample operating system
This project was a sample operating system coded in C. A simple implementation of some of the key components that modern operating systems employ. It included features such as demand paging, multithreaded task scheduling, and more. You can explore the code behind it here.
Light at the End
During the early months of the pandemic a group of friends that I met while at the National High School Game Academy at Carnegie Mellon University organized a game jam. In a team of three, Light at the End was our creation. A witty point and click adventure game where a player must make decisions, trade resources, and travel through a post apocalyptic New York City Subway system. We used Unity game engine to build it, check it out here.
Books
I believe the books I read can give people further insight into me, my interests, and my goals so I thought I’d include some of the most impactful readings I've read here.
Designing The Mind: Principles of Psychitecture by Ryan A. Bush
After taking an operating systems class at McGill University and comparing it with the psychology I have learned from my minor I began to see more and more parallels between a computer's operating system and the human brain. This book provides a framework of human psychology as if it were just that, an operating system, and how we can reprogram our mind to think, work, and feel better than ever before.
Mans Search for Meaning by Viktor E. Frankl
Viktor Frankl is a psychiatrist who survived the Nazi concentration camps during the Holocaust which helped him formulate his theory of Logotherapy which states that we require meaning and purpose in life to be psychologically healthy. This taught me about what pursuits in life are worth it and how one can achieve meaning through their actions and relationships.
Atomic Habits by James Clear
As a software engineer the question of “How can I optimize this code?” arises often. Atomic Habits answers this question but not for code, for ourselves. Learning how to create habits that occur automatically has allowed me to do many important daily tasks to better my health, increase my work output, and advance my learning all while freeing cognitive effort that I can direct towards more demanding tasks. The authors thesis of 1% improvement every day provides a simple recipe for improvement that compounds into significant change.
Why We Do What We Do by Edward L. Deci
I read this book for the course Human Motivation at McGill University. From this read I learned about the psychological conditions required for one to be intrinsically motivated and how we can create those conditions to help motivate others. This knowledge has been invaluable for motivating myself and for group projects with others to maintain motivation in a world where motivation can be quite sparse.
