Books
I believe the books I read can give people further insight into me, my interests, and my goals so I thought I would include some of the most impactful books I've read here.
You can keep up with what I'm currently reading on Goodreads.
Designing The Mind: Principles of Psychitecture by Ryan A. Bush
After taking an operating systems class at McGill University and comparing it with the psychology I have learned from my minor I began to see more and more parallels between a computer's operating system and the human brain. This book provides a framework of human psychology as if it were just that, an operating system, and how we can reprogram our mind to think, work, and feel better than ever before.
Mans Search for Meaning by Viktor E. Frankl
Viktor Frankl is a psychiatrist who survived the Nazi concentration camps during the Holocaust. His experience in the camps helped him to formulate his theory of Logotherapy, it states that we require meaning and purpose in life to be psychologically healthy. This taught me about what pursuits in life are worth doing and how one can achieve meaning through their actions and relationships.
Atomic Habits by James Clear
As a software engineer the question of “How can I optimize?” arises often. Atomic Habits answers this question but not just for code, but for ourselves too. Learning how to create habits that occur automatically has allowed me to do many important daily tasks to better my health, increase my work output, and advance my learning all while freeing cognitive effort that I can direct towards more demanding tasks. The authors thesis of 1% improvement every day provides a simple recipe for improvement that compounds into significant change.
Why We Do What We Do by Edward L. Deci
I read this book for the course Human Motivation at McGill University. From this book I learned about the psychological conditions required for one to be intrinsically motivated and how we can create those conditions to help motivate others. This knowledge has been invaluable for motivating myself and for group projects with others to maintain motivation in a world where it can be quite sparse.
